Back of the Napkin Video on APIs

The purpose of this assignment was to create a back of the napkin video that explain an engineering topic of my choice. I decided to create a video where I explained what web APIs are and some of their applications. I thought that I would be very uncomfortable with drawing and explaining my topic, but surprisingly I really enjoyed creating this video. I found that explaining what a web API is was much easier when I was able to create exactly what I was imagining, rather than finding an image that was only slightly similar. Being able to create my own drawings has also allowed me to utilize different metaphors to explain complex ideas about APIs, making it easier for the viewer to digest the information. Overall, this assignment has taught me to be more creative when it comes to communicating my ideas and that I should think outside the box more often to present information.

Reflection

The class Effective Engineering Communication had taught many different ways to communicate with my audience through different interactive exercises. These exercises emphasized the importance of effective communication and gave real life applications about when these communication methods would be used. This gave me more motivation to try and understand the content and to apply it to my everyday life. One specific lesson that stuck with me was the email writing lecture. I never really knew when it was appropriate to CC or BCC someone, so im glad I now know when it is appropriate to do so. I was also glad to learn about email writing etiquette as I always got nervous when sending emails. Overall, my time in ENG2003 has taught me a lot of things that are easily applicable to my everyday life.

Throughout the course of this semester, I have noticed that the way I interact with has changed. At the beginning of this course, my writing never had a target audience. For example, we had an assignment at the beginning of the year that required us to create a poster and a handout for a target audience. I struggled to find the right words to put on my poster and handout, so I ended up taking a lot longer than I anticipated to finish that assignment. As time progressed and I saw more examples, I began to understand how to write to specific audiences. This was evident when I created my back of the napkin video and noticed that I was able to explain more complicated concepts in a way that was more easily digestible. Because of all of the examples and attempts at writing to a targeted audience, I was able to focus more on creating the drawing part of the video, allowing me to have more fun creating it. I also noticed a difference in my body language at the end of the semester. I am now more aware of how I present myself when listening to someone. I now put away all my devices and lean forward slightly to show the other person that I am listening. I know that I will always use the different communication methods outside the classroom because I now realize how important it is to communicate effectively.
